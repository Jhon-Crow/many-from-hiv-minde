## Cryptocurrency (Primary Recommended Method)

### Legal Status in Russia (2025-2026)

Russia's approach to cryptocurrency has been evolving rapidly:

- Cryptocurrencies are classified as **property / digital financial assets** under Russian law
- They are **NOT legal tender** -- cannot be used for domestic payments within Russia
- **Exception (2024 law)**: digital currency is now **permitted for international trade settlements** as a way to navigate sanctions
- The Central Bank of Russia has proposed a comprehensive regulatory framework for crypto trading
- **Non-qualified investors**: limited to purchasing **300,000 RUB (~$3,300) per intermediary per year**, must pass a risk-awareness test
- **Qualified investors**: can trade without volume caps but also face a knowledge assessment
- Transactions exceeding **600,000 RUB must be reported** to tax authorities (Rosfinmonitoring)
- Russia is **Europe's largest crypto market**: approximately **$376B annual transaction volume**
- Regulatory framework expected to be completed by **July 1, 2026**
- Liability for illegal crypto operations will be introduced after this date

**Sources**:

- [Is Crypto Legal in Russia? Regulations & Compliance 2025](https://www.lightspark.com/knowledge/is-crypto-legal-in-russia)

### P2P Exchanges Available in Russia (Detailed)

#### Bybit P2P (TOP RECOMMENDATION)
- **URL**: https://www.bybit.com/
- **Status**: Fully operational for Russian users
- **RUB support**: Yes, via P2P marketplace
- **Payment methods accepted by sellers**: Tinkoff, Sberbank, SBP (Fast Payment System), YuMoney, MIR cards, UniCredit, Payeer
- **Commission**: Usually **zero** on P2P trades
- **Russian traffic share**: **26-31%** of total platform traffic (largest user base is Russian)
- **Escrow**: Yes -- trade protection through escrow system
- **KYC**: Required for P2P trading
- **How it works**: Buy USDT/BTC/ETH for RUB from sellers who accept bank transfers to Tinkoff/Sberbank. Sell USDT on P2P and receive RUB to your bank account.
- **Rating**: **Best choice** for Russian users converting crypto to/from RUB

## E-Wallets

### YooMoney (formerly Yandex.Money)
- **URL**: https://yookassa.ru/
- **Status**: Operational with limitations
- **International transfers**: Limited (USA transfers unavailable)
- **Best for**: Small transactions, domestic freelance payments
- **WARNING**: Strict new verification requirements since May 30, 2025 (most significant regulatory overhaul since 2014)
- Using YooMoney **without full ID verification** can now result in payment denials or automatic account flagging
- **Source**: [Russia's New Money Rules: What Expats, Freelancers & Crypto Users Must Know](https://www.americancrimea.site/p/russias-new-money-rules-what-expats)

### QIWI
- **Status**: **NOT operational** since February 2024
- **Reason**: Central Bank of Russia revoked QIWI Bank's license on February 21, 2024
- **Details**: Found to systematically violate anti-money laundering requirements
- **Impact**: Users lost the ability to make transactions; payment terminals stopped working
- **Source**: [QIWI - Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qiwi)

### WebMoney
- **Status**: Partially operational
- **Ruble wallets (WMR/WMP)**: **NOT available** -- bank license of KKB Bank JSC (guarantor) was revoked on February 11, 2022 by the Central Bank of Russia
- **Available wallets**: Dollar (WMZ), Euro (WME), Tenge, crypto wallets
- **Working functions**: Transfers between WebMoney wallets, P2P transfers to bank cards, exchange to cryptocurrencies, sending/receiving payments to foreign wallets and banks
- **Limitations**: Functionality may be limited due to sanctions
- **Source**: [WebMoney halts operations with Russian wallets - TASS](https://tass.com/economy/1401755)

### Sanctions Impact on Banking (2025-2026)
- Russia's national payment card system (NSPK) is now on the EU sanctions list
- EU extended transaction bans on the Russian National Card Payment System (MIR) and the Fast Payments System (SBP), taking effect **25 January 2026**
- This means Russian bank cards face increasing international restrictions
- **Source**: [EU Adopts 19th Russia Sanctions Package](https://www.skadden.com/insights/publications/2025/11/eu-adopts-19th-sanctions-package)

## Recommended Payment Flow for AI Agent (hive-mind) Earnings

### Primary Path: Crypto Bounty -> Bybit P2P -> Russian Bank

```
[Bounty Platform]                    [Crypto Wallet]                    [Exchange P2P]              [Bank Account]
 Gitcoin / GitEarn /     --USDC/-->   Non-custodial wallet   --USDT-->  Bybit P2P     --RUB-->     Tinkoff /
 Bountycaster / IssueHunt  ETH/SOL   (MetaMask / Trust)               (sell for RUB)              Sberbank
```

**Step-by-step:**
1. Hive-mind solves a GitHub issue on a bounty platform that pays in crypto
2. Bounty paid in USDC/USDT/ETH/SOL to a non-custodial crypto wallet
3. If needed, swap tokens to USDT on a DEX (Uniswap, Jupiter, etc.)
4. Transfer USDT to Bybit account
5. Sell USDT on Bybit P2P market for RUB
6. Buyer sends RUB directly to Tinkoff/Sberbank account via bank transfer or SBP

### Key Considerations
- **Tax reporting**: Crypto transactions over 600,000 RUB must be reported
- **Volume limits**: Non-qualified investors are limited to 300,000 RUB per intermediary per year
- **Bank account safety**: Use non-sanctioned banks (Tinkoff, Sberbank work for domestic P2P)
- **Wallet security**: Use non-custodial wallets; do not leave large amounts on exchanges
